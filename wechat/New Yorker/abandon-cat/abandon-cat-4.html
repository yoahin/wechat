My father belonged to the 20th Infantry Regiment第20步兵團 , which was part of the 16th Division第16師 ( Fushimi伏見区 Division). The nucleus主要戰力 of the 16th Division then consisted of four infantry regiments: the 9th Infantry Regiment (Kyoto), the 20th Infantry Regiment ( Fukuchiyama福知山 ), the 33rd Infantry Regiment ( Tsu City津市 , in Mie Prefecture三重県 ), and the 38th Infantry Regiment (Nara). It’s unclear why my father, who was from Kyoto City propera place called Kyoto City ㋡, was assigned not to the local 9th Regiment but instead to the far-off Fukuchiyama Regiment.

proper 緊跟一個名詞，强調這個名詞所指的事物本身。

At least this was how I understood it for the longest time, but when I looked more deeply into his background I found that I was wrong. In fact, my father belonged not to the 20th Infantry Regiment but to the 16th Transport Regiment, which was also part of the 16th Division. And this regiment wasn’t in Fukuchiyama but was headquartered in Fukakusa / Fushimi深草 / 伏見 , in Kyoto City. So why was I under the impression that my father had belonged to the 20th Infantry Regiment? I’ll discuss this point later㋡.

㋡Note

回答一個問題，又提出一個問題，讓讀者跟著自己的思緒走。行文節奏控制得很棒。

The 20th Infantry Regiment was known for being one of the first to arrive in Nanjing after the city fell. Military units from Kyoto were generally seen as well bred and urbane, but this particular regiment’s actions gave it a surprisingly bloody reputation. For a long time, I was afraid that my father had participated in the attack on Nanjing, and I was reluctant to investigate the details㋡. SP18He died, in August, 2008, at the age of ninety, without my ever having asked him about it, without his ever having talked about it.

My father was drafted in August of 1938. The 20th Infantry Regiment’s infamous march into Nanjing took place the previous year, in December of 1937, so my father had missed it by nearly a year. When I learned this, it was a tremendous relief, as if a great weight had been lifted㋡.

As a private second class二等兵 in the 16th Transport Regiment, my father boarded a troop transport in Ujina Harbor❉ on October 3, 1938, and arrived in Shanghai on October 6th. There his regiment joined up with the 20th Infantry Regiment. According to the Army’s wartime directory, the 16th Transport Regiment was primarily assigned to supply and security duties. If you follow the regiment’s movements, you see that it covered incredible distances for the time. For units that were barely motorized, and lacked sufficient fuel—horses were the main mode of transportation—travelling so far must have been extremely arduoustaking a lot of effort . SP3+1The situation at the front was dire: supplies couldn’t get there; there was a chronic shortage of rations(戰時給軍人的)口糧 and ammunition; the men’s uniforms were in tattersbadly worn ; and unsanitary conditions led to outbreaks of cholera霍亂 and other infectious diseases. It was impossible for Japan, with its limited strength, to control a huge country like China. Even though the Japanese Army was able to gain military control of one city after another, it was, practically speakingto tell the truth , incapable of occupying entire regions. The memoirs written by soldiers in the 20th Infantry Regiment give a clear picture of how pitiful the situation was. Transport troops were not usually directly involved in front-line fighting, but that didn’t mean they were safe. As they were only lightly armed (usually with just bayonets槍上的刺刀 ), when the enemy attacked from the rear they suffered major casualties.

During World War II, the Second General Army and Chūgoku Regional (中國地方，相對與日本的遠國和近國而言，并非指中華人民共和國) Army was headquartered in Hiroshima (広島市，就是後來遭美軍原子彈轟炸的廣島), and the Army Marine Headquarters was located at Ujina Harbor (宇品港，現広島港). The city also had large depots of military supplies, and was a key center for shipping.

Soon after starting at the Seizan school, my father had discovered the pleasures of haiku❉ and joined a haiku circle. He was really into it, to use a modern idiom. Several of the haiku he wrote while he was a soldier were published in the school’s haiku journal; most likely he mailed them to the school from the front:

Birds migrating
Ah—where they are headed
must be my homeland

A soldier, yet a priest
clasping my hands in prayer
toward the moon

❉WikipediA

Haiku (俳句) (plural haiku) is a short form of Japanese poetry in three phrases, typically characterized by three qualities:

  1. The essence of haiku is "cutting" (kiru). This is often represented by the juxtaposition of two images or ideas and a kireji ("cutting word") between them, a kind of verbal punctuation mark which signals the moment of separation and colours the manner in which the juxtaposed elements are related.
  2. Traditional haiku often consist of 17 on (音) (also known as morae (duration of a short syllable) though often loosely translated as "syllables"), in three phrases of 5, 7, and 5 on, respectively.
  3. A kigo (seasonal reference, 季語), usually drawn from a saijiki (year-time chronicle 歳時記), an extensive but defined list of such terms.

I’m no haiku expert, so it’s beyond me to say how accomplishedvery skilled his were㋡. Clearly, what holds these poems together is not technique but the open, honest feelings that underscore them.

My father had been studying, no doubt conscientiously, to become a priest. But a simple clerical error had turned him into a soldier. He went through brutal basic training, was handed a Type 38 rifle, placed on a troop-transport ship, and sent off to the fearsome battles at the front. His unit was constantly on the move, clashing with Chinese troops and guerrillas游擊隊 who put up a fierce resistance. In every way imaginable, this was the opposite of life in a peaceful temple in the Kyoto hills. He must have suffered tremendous mental confusion and spiritual turmoil. In the midst of all that, writing haiku may have been his sole consolation. ☈Things he never could have written in his letters, or theythings that wouldn’t have made it past the censors, he put into the form of haiku—expressing himself in a symbolic code, as it were—where he was able to honestly bare his true feelings㋡.

☈Paraphrase

He put things (experiences, feelings, and so) into the form of haiku, for two reasons:

1. he never could have written them in his letters;
2. even if he could, the cencors wouldn’t allow the content to pass.
