Until 1789 the biggest Christmas killjoys were Christians themselves. ThatThe situation changed with the French revolution, which aimed to sweep away God, tradition and superstition and replace与 sweep 并列 it❶ with a regime founded on rationalism, enquiry and progress. Christmas didn’t stand a chancesurvive the revolution. It became just another day under the new revolutionary calendar❉, which was used in France between 1793 and 1805.❷ It wasn’t even on December 25th anymore: the new calendar named months based on the seasons;[1] the ten days of the week❸ were dedicated not to saints but agricultural tools, rocks, plants and animals.❹ The day formerly known as Christmas became the 10th of Nivôse (month of snow), Day of the Dog.❺[2][3]

❷法国新历只短暂地于1793到1805年间用过（下面维基也提到了）。实际上，1793年10月24日开始使用，这一年是 Year II of the Republic。

❸法国新历中，一年被分为秋、冬、春、夏四个季节，每个季节包含三个月。圣诞节所在的月份是新历冬季的第一个月，是每一年的第四个月，叫做 Nivôse（源自拉丁语 nivosus = snowy）。按照当前的公历，它是12月22日前后开始，1月20日前后结束。

❹新历中，每个月记为30天，然后等分为三周，每周十天（称作 decade）。每一轮的第十日叫做 décadi，为休息日。做每天有固定的主题，农具（通常为以0结尾的日期）、石头、植物、动物（通常为以5结尾的日期）等。

每一年多出来的日子，在法国新历中归到了最后一个月 Fructidor（源自拉丁语 fructus, "fruits"），被用来做全国假期。按照当前公历，这个月于每年8月18或19日开始，因为这个月多几天，所以下一年的第一个月 Vendémiaire (源自拉丁语 vindemia, vintage)是9月23日前后才开始。

French Republican calendar

The French Republican calendar (French: calendrier républicain français), also commonly called the French Revolutionary calendar (calendrier révolutionnaire français), was a calendar created and implemented during the French Revolution, and used by the French government for about 12 years from late 1793 to 1805, and for 18 days by the Paris Commune in 1871. The revolutionary system was designed in part to remove all religious and royalist influences from the calendar, and was part of a larger attempt at decimalisation in France (which also included decimal time of day, decimalisation of currency, and metrication).
